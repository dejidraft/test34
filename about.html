About Method-of-Mind

Method-of-Mind is a student-friendly psychology study toolkit built around clear explanations, exam-ready organization, and practical examples—designed by a real student, not auto-generated.

What you’ll find here

Short concept guides, mini practice prompts, and clean “method-first” breakdowns that help you understand how psychology works (and how to write about it).



Design philosophy

  • Simple language first, then technical terms
  • Definitions + examples + “how to use it” in essays/tests
  • Skimmable layouts made for tired students

What this is

Method-of-Mind is a psychology study toolkit designed to help students understand concepts clearly, apply them correctly, and study with structure.

This project focuses on course-agnostic psychology foundations — the ideas that show up again and again across exams, essays, and research discussions.

Why it exists

Many psychology resources explain what a concept is, but not how to use it. Method of Mind is built to bridge that gap.

  • Clear definitions in plain language
  • Examples that mirror exam questions
  • Common confusions called out explicitly
  • Practice prompts that force application

How to use it

  1. Start with a topic overview.
  2. Read the key concepts and examples.
  3. Answer the practice questions before checking explanations.
  4. Use the study sheets to review.

Who this is for

Method-of-Mind is designed for undergraduate psychology students, especially those studying:

  • Introductory psychology
  • Research methods and statistics
  • Biopsychology / physiological psychology
  • Personality psychology

It’s also useful for anyone who wants a structured way to revisit core psychology concepts.
